The Wii Nunchuck App

Instructions for usage

This application allows you to synchronize your iPhone or iPod Touch with a Wii Nunchuck. The pairing requires the use of an Arduino micro-controller, and several other pieces of hardware before the devices will be fully in sync (a full parts list is available here).

Once your setup is assembled, your iPhone can be controlled by the data stream sent out from a Wii Nunchuck, which includes accelerometer information in 3 axes, joystick information in 2 axes, and the use of the Z and C buttons. This data can be mapped to control a variety of sounds, visual queues, or anything else that is accessible through the iPhone SDK.



This app was created to attempt to bridge the gap between the conventional use of a touch-screen device and more intuitive methods of control. Designed for a DSP for Embedded Devices course at the University of Miami, focus went into making the app AV-savvy, but there is no limit as to what an input stream of data from an auxiliary control can provide for a creative user.

The method of data transfer is carried though the headphone jack into the line-in input of an iPhone. The protocol was designed and implemented by Eddie Bertot and myself for this (and other) iPhone-related projects. This method is assuredly a work-around from the standardized collection of approved iPhone accessories, and is essentially a hack to get data into the device without the use of the 30-pin connector. For this reason, the data stream may be slightly noisy. Ideally, updates in the future will improve the fidelity of information sent into the device.

For further background information and instructions on this application, the source-code, or detailed documentation on the system, check out the website for this project. Thanks, and hope you enjoy!
